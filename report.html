Section 01 · The full protocol

The Protocol

A cephalometric reading of eleven features — soft-tissue measurements compared against established research on facial harmony. The findings are empirical guidelines rather than exact measurements, and the aim throughout is refinement of what already works, not reinvention.

Approach

The analysis was approached from a cephalometric point of view: taking soft-tissue measurements and comparing them with established scientific research on facial aesthetics. This keeps observations less subjective — though the recommendations remain empirical guidelines, not precise measurements.

Limitations

A neutral head position cannot be guaranteed, which can affect under-jaw and submental readings; lighting and camera quality influence qualitative assessment. A full assessment would require radio-cephalographs, which were not available. This report is not a medical diagnosis.

Hair

Hair style

From the front your hair is already thick and wavy, which gives strong framing but can add a little bulk around the temples and ears. Moving to a shorter cut that removes some side volume while keeping length on top will clean up the silhouette and place more focus on the eyes and jaw. A side parting lets the top sweep across the forehead, breaking the vertical height slightly and adding a controlled, masculine wave. A light sea-salt spray, scrunched into the lengths while drying, defines texture without stiffness — casual yet intentional through the day.

BeforeAfter

Hair loss

Based on your photographs, we estimate a current Norwood stage 1 of hairline recession, so the focus is prevention. The hairline shows only minimal recession at the temples and frontal-scalp density is high, so there are no clear signs of active pattern loss. The M-shaped contour reads as a youthful masculine feature rather than thinning. At this stage the priority is simply maintaining scalp health with consistent cleansing and avoiding excessive heat or harsh bleaching — there are no non-surgical loss treatments worth pursuing here, and attention is better spent on styling that highlights this natural advantage.

Hair health

Because your follicles are tightly packed and the strands sit in a balanced range between dry and oily, overall hair health is a real strength. Regular trims, gentle shampoo and an occasional nourishing conditioner will preserve volume, reduce surface frizz and keep the waves responsive.

Refining length, adopting a structured side part and enhancing natural wave with a sea-salt product frames your masculine features more cleanly while preserving strong native density.

Brows & Eyes

Eyebrows

You already have dark, dense brows that sit fairly low and give a masculine frame to the eyes. Targeted threading along the upper edge and outer tails removes stray hairs without lifting the brow or thinning the body — keeping the natural sloped shape but making the outline cleaner. A touch of dark brow gel through the mid-section increases density and contrast so the arch looks neater while still natural.

Eyelashes

Your lashes are already moderately dense and clearly visible along the upper lid, giving a defined border to the almond eyes without appearing styled. With no gaps or very short lengths, serums or extensions aren't necessary — simple maintenance like gently cleansing the lash line and avoiding harsh rubbing is enough.

Eyes

Your eyes project a steady almond shape with a neutral tilt and clear iris definition — a calm yet slightly piercing gaze. Mild surface redness can appear after long days or screen time, softening the clarity of the sclera. For days when a sharper, more rested look is wanted, whitening eye drops used sparingly as directed can brighten the whites. Coupled with good sleep and hydration, this keeps the eye region clean, focused and youthful.

Under eye

The under-eye area is good for your age, with only mild hollowness and soft grey-tan pigmentation. Daily use of a hydrating eye serum with hyaluronic acid will plump the skin slightly and soften the tear-trough groove; a vitamin C eye serum can gradually brighten pigment and support collagen. On busy days, a light peach-based colour-correcting concealer patted only where the shadow is deepest looks more rested without obvious makeup.

Gentle brow grooming, subtle colour correction, hydration-focused eye care and occasional whitening drops refine an already strong eye region — clearer, more rested and slightly sharper, without feminising it.

Jaw structure

From the front, the mandibular border sweeps in a smooth U-shape with standard male width, so the lower third looks solid and grounded rather than narrow. The vertical rise from chin up to the angle is tall, lengthening the sides of the face and adding an athletic impression. Further enhancement

Skin

The condition of your facial skin is a key part of overall appearance and one of the main signals of youth.

Skincare protocol

Overall your skin is youthful, with very few lines and only minor texture — mainly a slightly rougher chin and mild grain on the forehead. Oil production is higher through the nose, cheeks and beard area, which can enlarge the look of pores and add diffuse redness. With no significant acne or scarring, the aim is to preserve this advantage while evening colour and refining texture. Research on male faces shows that smoother, more even-toned skin strongly increases perceived health and attractiveness.

A simple daily routine works well. Morning and night, a gentle foaming cleanser removes oil and pollution without stripping the barrier. After cleansing, a toner for combination skin balances pH and lightly hydrates. A vitamin C serum then boosts radiance, supports collagen and gradually reduces background discolouration. In the evening, a retinol product in the 0.25–0.5% range, started three nights a week and increased only as tolerated. Finish with a moisturiser containing niacinamide or hyaluronic acid, then each morning apply factor-50 sunscreen as the final step.

BeforeAfter

Further skin enhancement

Once the baseline routine feels comfortable, a few steps can refine the complexion further. For persistent redness and mild pigmentation on the nose, cheeks and beard area, the report suggests Intense Pulsed Light treatment with a dermatologist to even colour and shrink superficial vessels. Short courses of topical azelaic acid — or, under medical supervision, hydroquinone — can reduce patchy pigmentation and post-inflammatory marks. Occasional Hydra Facials cleanse, exfoliate and infuse serums without significant downtime.

In-clinic options. IPL, Hydra Facials and prescription topicals are non-invasive but involve a clinic or prescription. They appear in your plan only under Optional, flagged to discuss with a professional.

With this routine and selected professional treatments, you can keep already-youthful skin while building a more even, calm and refined surface that enhances every other feature.

Closing Recommendations

Taken together, the analysis shows a strong masculine framework — an oval face, clear jaw and dense hair — so the goal is refinement rather than major change. Shortening the haircut and moving toward a controlled side part reduces excess bulk at the temples and places more visual weight on the eyes and jaw, while a light sea-salt spray keeps soft waves looking natural but intentional. Because density and recession are excellent, no specialised hair-loss treatment is needed.

Around the eyes, small adjustments make the gaze look more rested and focused: cleaning the upper and outer brow borders by threading while keeping their low position, a touch of dark brow gel, occasional whitening drops when the sclera looks red, and daily hyaluronic acid and vitamin C under the eyes, with a light peach colour-corrector only where the shadow is deepest.

In the central face, existing high cheekbones and lean tissue already give a sculpted look; gradually reducing body fat reveals this structure and sharpens the jaw. A shorter, tidy beard that follows the natural mandibular border, with the neck kept clean, showcases the angle. Parallel neck training through extensions and curls adds muscular thickness so the jaw looks even better supported from every angle.

Skin care is the other main pillar: a gentle cleanser morning and night, then toner, vitamin C serum, moisturiser and daily factor-50 sunscreen, with careful retinol at night introduced slowly to stimulate collagen and keep pores clear. For stubborn redness and pigmentation, topical azelaic acid and medically supervised options can be layered in as advised. Staying consistent over months lets each feature move a little closer to the target while keeping you recognisably yourself and firmly within a natural, masculine aesthetic.
